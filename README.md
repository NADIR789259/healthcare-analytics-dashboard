# 🏥 Apollo Healthcare Analytics Dashboard


## 📌 Project Overview
This project features a comprehensive interactive dashboard designed for Apollo Healthcare. It provides a high-level executive view of the hospital's operational, financial, and clinical performance over a one-year period. By visualizing key metrics, hospital administration can identify seasonal trends, optimize resource allocation, and monitor financial health.

## 📊 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
At a glance, the dashboard tracks the following macro-level metrics:
* **Total Patients:** 7,157 (7.157K)
* **Total Billing:** $190 Million
* **Total Insurance Covered:** $171.39 Million (Indicating a high rate of insured patients)
* **Average Billing per Patient:** $27K

## 📈 Key Insights & Analysis

### 1. Patient Admission Trends
* Admissions show high seasonality, peaking significantly in January (1,091 patients) and gradually declining through the spring and summer months before stabilizing in the ~450-500 range during Q3 and Q4.
* **Actionable Insight:** Staffing and resource scaling should be maximized during the Q1 winter peak.

### 2. Financials by Diagnosis
* Viral Infections are the leading driver of billing revenue, accounting for $53M.
* This is followed by Flu ($46M) and Malaria ($38M).
* Pneumonia accounts for the lowest relative billing in the top 5 at $15M.

### 3. Resource Utilization
* **Bed Occupancy:** Private beds are in the highest demand (3.58K), followed by General wards (2.39K) and ICU beds (1.19K).
* **Diagnostic Tests:** Blood Tests (2.2K) and MRIs (1.8K) are the most frequently conducted procedures, highlighting a heavy reliance on laboratory and advanced imaging departments.

### 4. Staff Performance & Patient Satisfaction
* **Doctor Performance:** The top 5 performing doctors (led by Mark Joy with 1,023 cases) show an incredibly balanced distribution of workload, with almost identical patient/case counts.
* **Patient Feedback:** Feedback scores are evenly distributed across four tiers (3.50, 4.00, 4.90, and 5.00), showing generally positive patient sentiment.
